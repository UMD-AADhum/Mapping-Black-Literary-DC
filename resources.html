Archival Collections

In addition to web archives of the Washington Post and individual venue webpages retrieved from InternetArchive.org, Mapping Black Literary DC consulted the following physical and digital archival collections:



Brian Gilmore Papers ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2257

This collection contains articles, essays, poetry, research material, correspondence and other materials relating to the works of Washington D.C. poet Brian Gilmore. The material dates from 1968-2023.


E. Ethelbert Miller Papers ↳

George Washington University Libraries

CCollection Identifier: MS2215

A meticulous record keeper, Ethelbert Miller has amassed correspondence, fliers, posters, journals, photographs, interviews, and articles pertaining to his career ranging from 1936-2022 with the bulk covering 1962-2018.


Essex Hemphill and Wayson Jones Collection ↳

New York Public Library - Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture

Collection Identifier: Sc MG 832

This collection contains articles, essays, poetry, research material, correspondence and other materials relating to the works of Washington D.C. poet Brian Gilmore. The material dates from 1968-2023.


Grace Cavalieri Papers ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2007

Collection contains audio cassettes, CD-ROMs, literary journals, anthologies, plays, video cassettes, and magnetic audiotape reels produced between 1976 and 2000s. The audio recordings are of local poets that Grace Cavalieri interviewed on her radio program The Poet and the Poem and an earlier radio show titled Poetry from the City. These shows date from the middle 1970s through the present.


The Hilltop Digital Archive ↗

Howard University Moorland-Spingarn Research Center

Collection Identifier: N/A

The Hilltop is the student newspaper of Howard University. Founded in 1924 by famed author Zora Neale Hurston, the paper has documented the voice of the student population on campus. Over its 94 years of history the paper has documented almost every major event of the 20th century.


Holly Bass Poetry Ephemera Collection ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2224

This collection contains postcards and fliers related to poetry readings and other performances by Holly Bass and other local poets and writers.


Kim Roberts Biographical Files of DC Poets ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2222

This collection contains programs, correspondence, articles and other miscellanies amassed by D.C. poet Kim Roberts pertaining to over 250 Washington DC poets.


Kwame Alexander Papers ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2222

This collection contains documents from Kwame Alexander and his two publishing companies - BlackWords Press and Alexander Publishing Group. This collection also contains Kwame Alexander's website, added in 2023. The collection ranges in dates from 1990 until 2023.


Marita Golden Papers ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2235

The material in this collection consists of manuscript drafts, galleys, and research notes related to the work of author and teacher Marita Golden. The collection also includes archived versions of the Marita Golden website. The material dates from 1969-2023.


Toni Asante Lightfoot Poetry Ephemera Collection ↗

George Washington University Libraries

Collection Identifier: MS2233

This collection contains postcards, fliers, and poems related to poetry readings and other performances by Toni Asante Lightfoot and other local poets and writers.


U Street Oral History Project ↗

DC Public Library The People’s Archive

Collection Identifier: OHP026

Oral history interview with Marita Golden, an African-American woman who recounts her childhood in Columbia Heights and Foggy Bottom neighborhoods. She describes working as a journalist for the American University newspaper when she was a freshman there and going to cover the devastation of the U Street Corridor after the riots. Ms. Golden recalls becoming a community activist in the U Street area following the riots. She volunteered for Marion Barry's organization, Pride Inc. and for The Freedom School. She was also in the neighborhood to take classes at Howard University. In the late 1960s, Ms. Golden discovered poetry and was able to get her first poem included in the anthology Today's Negro Voices.


UMD Student Newspapers, 1910-2016

University of Maryland Special Collections and University Archives ↗

Collection Identifier: N/A

The UMD Student Newspapers database is an effort to digitize and make accessible the major student newspapers produced on the College Park campus. The UMD Student Newspapers database contains issues of The Diamondback and its seven predecessor papers, Black Explosion, The Eclipse, Expression, Ha-Koach, Mitzpeh, Hanoori, Public Asian and three predecessor papers, and La Voz Latina.


Washingtoniana Periodicals Collection ↗

DC Public Library The People’s Archive

Collection Identifier: Periodicals

Consulted periodicals include Blacklight, an independent newspaper serving the Black gay community of Washington, DC; Washington Blade, an independent newspaper serving the LGBTQIA+ community; and Women in the Life, a magazine centering Black lesbians covering topics related to art, politics, literature, history, relationships, family, and sexuality.


Wayson Jones Collections, 1990-1994 ↗

Rainbow History Project

Collection Identifier: N/A

The Wayson Jones Collections include articles from BLK magazine relating to the African American gay experience in Washington, DC and flyers and a postcard advertising performances by Wayson Jones and his partner Essex Hemphill.


Related Projects


Black Broadway on U ↗

Launched in February 2014, the Black Broadway on U: A Transmedia Project is a groundbreaking multi-platform story and public history initiative created to amplify, chronicle, preserve and enhance, the under-told story, cultural legacy, local memories and voices of Washington, D.C.’s marginalized Black community along the historic greater U Street community when it was known as “Black Broadway”, a city within a city.


Cultural Tourism DC City Within A City: Greater U Street Heritage Trail ↗

Follow the signs on this self-guided Cultural Tourism DC Neighborhood Heritage Trail to learn more about Washington's Greater U Street neighborhood, where a nationally significant, self-sufficient African American community flourished in the early 20th century.


DC Writers’ Homes ↗

This project documents the homes of literary authors who once lived in the greater Washington, DC region. DCWH includes novelists, poets, playwrights, and memoirists from the city’s founding in 1800 through the present.


Rainbow History Project - Places and Spaces ↗

A map and data set featuring locations of importance to DC area LGBTQ communities.


Remembering YoU ↗

Remembering YoU is a community-driven archival initiative created to preserve U Street’s rich history and empower local residents to retake control of the narratives shaping their histories and neighborhoods.


Further Research

For current information about the people and publications behind the archival collections used in the creation of this project, we encourage you to visit the below sites.


Beltway Poetry Quarterly ↗

Beltway Poetry Quarterly is an award-winning online literary journal and resource bank that originated in Washington, DC and the surrounding Mid-Atlantic region. We are now a global beltway, encircling the epicenters of major metropolises everywhere.


E. Ethelbert Miller ↗

E. Ethelbert Miller is a writer and literary activist. From 1974-2000 he was the founder and director of the Ascension Poetry Reading Series which presented hundreds of African American poets and poets of color to the general public. The author of several collections of poetry, he has written two memoirs, Fathering Words: The Making of An African American Writer (2000) and The 5th Inning (2009). His poetry anthology In Search of Color Everywhere was awarded the PEN Oakland Josephine Miles Award in 1994.


Holly Bass ↗

Holly Bass is a multidisciplinary performance and visual artist, writer and director. Her visual art work includes photography, installation, video and performance and can be found in the collections of the Corcoran Gallery of Art and the DC Art Bank, as well as private collections.


Hurston/Wright Foundation ↗

Co-founded by Marita Golden, the Hurston/Wright Foundation’s mission is to provide services, support and opportunities that mentor, recognize and provide community for professional and aspiring Black writers.


Kim Roberts ↗

An award-winning poet and literary historian residing in Washington, DC. Author of six books of poems, editor of two anthologies, and co-editor of the web exhibit DC Writers’ Homes.


Women in the Life Association ↗

Women in the Life Association is a social justice organization advancing the rights of lesbians and queer women of color through cultural affirmation, education and advocacy. WITLA published 87 issues of Women in the Life Magazine between 1993 and 2003.
